Section 1.1: What Are ICS and SCADA Systems?

Industrial Control Systems (ICS) are programmable systems and devices that interact with the physical environment to monitor and control industrial processes. ICS includes Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems, Distributed Control Systems (DCS), and other configurations commonly used in industrial sectors and critical infrastructure.

These systems rely on components such as Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs), sensors, actuators, and Human-Machine Interfaces (HMIs) to regulate and optimize operations.

Key Components of ICS/SCADA Systems:

  • PLCs: Specialized computers that execute control logic for machinery and processes
  • RTUs: Devices that collect data from sensors and transmit it to central systems
  • HMIs: Interfaces that allow operators to monitor and control processes
  • Sensors and Actuators: Devices that measure physical parameters (e.g., temperature, pressure) and execute actions

Section 1.2: ICS/SCADA Environments

ICS/SCADA systems are deployed in various critical infrastructure environments, including:

Power Grids

Managing electricity generation, transmission, and distribution across regional and national networks.

Water Treatment Plants

Monitoring and controlling water purification and distribution processes for municipal and industrial use.

Section 1.3: The Purdue Model

The Purdue Model is a hierarchical framework for segmenting ICS/SCADA systems into levels, ranging from enterprise systems (Level 5) to physical processes (Level 0). This segmentation helps isolate critical systems and reduce risk.

Level 5: Enterprise IT Systems - Business applications, ERP systems
Level 4: Site-wide Operations - Plant-wide monitoring and coordination
Level 3: Operations Management - Production scheduling and optimization
Level 2: Supervisory Control - SCADA systems and HMIs
Level 1: Control Systems - PLCs, RTUs, and basic control
Level 0: Physical Processes - Sensors, actuators, and physical equipment

Section 2.1: Common Threats to ICS/SCADA Systems

ICS/SCADA systems face unique threats due to their reliance on legacy technologies and their critical role in infrastructure. Understanding these threats is essential for proper defense.

Malware

Software designed to disrupt operations, steal data, or gain unauthorized access to control systems.

Insider Threats

Malicious actions by employees, contractors, or other trusted individuals with system access.

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts data and demands payment for decryption, potentially halting operations.

Section 2.2: Vulnerabilities in ICS/SCADA Systems

ICS/SCADA systems are particularly vulnerable due to several inherent characteristics:

Lack of Encryption

Many ICS protocols transmit data in plaintext, making communications vulnerable to interception and manipulation.

Weak Authentication

Default passwords, shared accounts, and lack of multi-factor authentication create significant security gaps.

Network Exposure

ICS systems often connect to external networks, increasing attack surfaces and potential entry points.

Section 2.3: Adversary TTPs in OT Environments

Adversaries targeting operational technology (OT) environments employ specific tactics, techniques, and procedures:

  • Pivoting: Moving from enterprise IT systems to ICS networks through compromised connections
  • Protocol Exploitation: Exploiting ICS-specific protocols (e.g., Modbus, DNP3) that lack security features
  • Physical Impact: Manipulating physical processes to cause damage, disruption, or safety hazards
